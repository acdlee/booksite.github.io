About the Book

The Diary of a Wimpy Kid is a children's book first published in 2007, although it was released on the educational browser game website Funbrain in 2004. The story follows a young teenager named Greg Heffley on his journey through the ubiquitous struggles of middle school. We tag alone through Greg's journal, as he depicts and reflects the standout events of his daily life.

Traveling through early adolescene, Greg must circumnavigate interpersonal relationships with his friends and family, along with his bullies and peers. This story not only serves as a pertinent companion for those in Greg's age bracket, but also an opportunity to reflect and learn for those of us who've forgotten our similar undergoings and pre-adulthood origins.

Plot

Greg's First Day

The story begins with Greg's first day of middle school, where we find him writing in the journal that he so hypocritically disavows. Immediately, we see Greg's perception of the world around him: a temporary hell swarmed by morons and people not worthy of himself. A day later, Greg finds himself facing a moldy piece of cheese on a basketball court. This particular piece is notorious: any who touch the cheese is instantly infected with the "Cheese Touch" and will be ostracized by their peers. Soonafter we meet Rodrick, Greg's bully of an older brother, who seems to live under different rules than those set for Greg by his parents.

Greg Meets Rowley

The weekend following the first week of school, Greg heads over to play hangout with his friend Rowley. Their friendship is quite shaky: Greg views Rowley as less than himself, and even recycles Rodrick's pranks on Rowley, despite Greg considering him, techincally, his best friend. Despite Greg's concern with Rowley's effect on his image, Greg relies Rowley for his friendship, which becomes more apparent to Greg as time goes on.

Greg Runs for Office

After realizing the potential "social cred" achievable through status, Greg decides to pursue the Treasurer position in the school government. Greg's first campgain strategy is to sling mud (wonder where he learned that from), which of course is not allowed (in middle school), and quickly retires from school politics.

Greg Goes Trick or Treating

The next big event in the journal is Halloween. After visiting a haunted house, an inspired Greg draws up a plan to create his own in Rowley's basement, along with posters to spread word. Things don't go as planned and Rowley ends up grounded, which is only concerning to Greg because "now where am I[Greg] supposed to play my video games?".

Greg vs Kindergarders

As the year progresses, Greg continually looks for opportunities to self-serve, mostly at the expense of others. Being the usual recipient of Greg's narcassism, Rowley finally reaches a boiling point with Greg during their stint as Safety Patrols (which Greg is really only in for the hot cocoa). While Rowley was taking a quiz in forth period, Greg walks home a group of kindergarders. As reported by a bystander, Greg was ""terrorizing"" the kindergardners. However, Rowley recieves the blame and week-suspension punishment instead of Greg, over a misconception.

Greg and Rowley Split

After confessing to Rowley and simulatenously trying to inject him with "lesson learned" rhetoric, the two's friendship splits. Rowley wises up and tells the truth to the head of the Safety Patrols, which leaves Greg feeling backstabbed by his friend, furthering their split.

Greg has an Allergy

The two trial new friends seperately for a few months, only to return to each other in conflict. Greg confronts Rowley over a comic he believes they co-created. Rowley disagrees, and the peanut gallery instigates a fight between the former best friends. Their fight is interupted by a group of older teenagers they pranked and evaided on Halloween. Then, the unthinkable happens: Rowley is forced to eat THE cheese by himself (since Greg lied and said he had an allergy) and promptly was infected.

Greg's Resolution

In the day that followed, the school was fierce to discover what happened to the missing cheese. For the first time in the book, Greg shows some maturity. Instead of waiting for someone to discover it was Rowley who touched (and ate) the cheese, Greg decides to claim the Cheese Touch and protect Rowley.

About the Author

Jeff Kinney, an author and cartoonist, was born in Maryland in 1971. During his time at the University of Maryland, he ran a comic strip for the Diamondback (a popular newspaper for the University) called "Igdoof".

After not finding more success with his comic strips, Jeff began writing the initial ideas for Diary of a Wimpy Kid, which would later go on to become a best selling book, recognized globally for its bestselling story of Greg Heffley.

Today, Jeff resides in Massachusetts with his family. As a family, they own and operate An Unlikely Story, a book store in Plainville, Massachusetts.
